title: "What I look for in a first SDR interview"
date: "Mar 2025"
tag: "Hiring"
summary: "The signals that tell me someone will figure it out — vs. someone who will wait to be told."
---
I've sat across from a lot of SDR candidates. The ones who become great AEs share one thing: they're learners who don't need permission to learn.

Here's what I actually look for in a first interview:

**Curiosity about the problem, not the product.** I ask them to sell me something they personally use. The ones who ask me questions before pitching — even for 60 seconds — are the ones I pay attention to. Selling before understanding is a habit that's very hard to unlearn.

**Evidence of self-directed improvement.** Not "I read sales books." Specifically: what did you read, what did you try, what happened? I want to see the loop — not just the input.

**Comfort with rejection without resentment.** I tell them I'm going to say no to something they propose and watch how they respond. Whether they get curious about the no or just accept it and move on.

**Coachability vs. compliance.** Compliant reps do what you say. Coachable reps understand why you said it and apply the principle elsewhere.

What I don't look for: previous SDR experience, specific industry knowledge, perfect articulation. Those are learnable. The above isn't.
