---
title: "The difference between a good AE and a great one"
date: "Feb 2025"
tag: "Management"
summary: "It's not about closing. It's about what they do when the deal isn't going well."
---
I've worked alongside enough AEs now to see the pattern clearly.

Good AEs close deals. Great AEs control deals.

The difference shows up in three places:

**When the deal goes quiet.** A good AE follows up. A great AE diagnoses. "Gone quiet" is information — it means something changed. Great AEs go back and find out what changed, who changed it, and whether the deal is still alive or just hasn't been declared dead yet.

**When the champion loses power.** Good AEs start over. Great AEs had already multi-threaded before it happened — not because they were paranoid but because they understood that champion and economic buyer are almost never the same person.

**When they get a no.** Good AEs accept the no and move on. Great AEs accept the no and understand it. "Not right now" means something different than "not this product" which means something different than "not you." Great AEs know which no they got.
