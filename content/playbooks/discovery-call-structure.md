Discovery is the most abused word in sales. Everyone says they do it. Almost nobody does it well.

Here's my actual structure. Five questions, always in this order:

**Line 1 — "What made you take this call today?"** Not why did you book it, but today. Forces them to articulate the trigger. The answer tells you urgency and pain severity in one shot.

**Line 2 — "Walk me through how this problem shows up in your day-to-day."** Operational pain, not conceptual pain. I want specifics. If they can't give me a specific, the pain isn't real enough yet.

**Line 3 — "What have you tried before?"** Tells me what they've already rejected, what their internal biases are, and whether I'm selling against a past failed attempt. Critical for positioning.

**Line 4 — "Who else feels this problem?"** This is my multi-threading entry point. I'm not asking to meet others yet. I'm mapping the org through their answer.

**Line 5 — "What does good look like in 6 months?"** Their definition of success, not mine. Everything after this is about connecting my product to that specific outcome.

The sequence matters. You build credibility through questions 1-3 before you map the org or talk outcomes. Jump to 4 or 5 too early and you sound like every other rep.
