Most cold emails are about the sender. Mine are about the recipient's world.

The structure I use every time, in order:

**Line 1 — The observation.** Something specific I noticed about their company, role, or industry. Not "I see you're VP of Sales at X." Something that required 5 minutes of actual research.

**Line 2 — The pattern.** A problem I've seen in companies like theirs, stated as a pattern, not a pitch. "Companies at your stage often find that X creates Y friction" not "our product solves Y."

**Line 3 — The ask.** One question. Not a demo request. Not a calendar link. A question they can answer in one sentence that either confirms or denies the pattern.

A real example (anonymised):

"Noticed your team recently expanded into the enterprise segment. Companies making that transition often find their document workflow becomes a bottleneck — compliance reviews slow down contracting at the worst possible moment.

Is that something you're running into?"

That's it. 47 words. The reply rate on this specific email was 31% over 60 sends. The meeting rate from replies was 68%.

The discipline is leaving out everything you want to say.
