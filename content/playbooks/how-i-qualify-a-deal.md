---
title: "How I qualify a deal before investing time"
date: "Feb 2025"
tag: "Qualification"
summary: "My Sandler-influenced checklist for deciding whether a deal deserves my calendar."
---
I used to chase everything that moved. Pipeline looked healthy. Win rate was embarrassing.

Now I run every opportunity through four questions before I invest more than one meeting:

**Pain** — Can they articulate a specific problem with a cost attached? Not "we'd like to improve X." What does not solving this cost them in revenue, time, or risk? If they can't name it, I'm not qualified yet.

**Power** — Am I talking to someone who can say yes, or someone who can only say no? I need at least one conversation with an economic buyer before I build a proposal. Not after.

**Fit** — Is our product actually the right answer for their stage, size, and use case? I've walked away from deals where we could have won but shouldn't have. Bad-fit customers churn and damage your reputation.

**Timeline** — Is there an event forcing a decision? A renewal, a compliance deadline, a new hire, a board commitment? Without an external forcing function, "we're evaluating" means never.

If I can't answer all four, the next step is always to get those answers — not to move forward as if I already have them.
